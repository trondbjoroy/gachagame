# Mainnet economy model — working draft

**Status: exploratory modeling. Nothing here is a commitment; all numbers
are under study for the mainnet design.** Generated by
`scripts/economy_model.py`, seeded with testnet behavior (PostHog week of
2026-07-07, on-chain aggregates from the testnet arena contract).

## The design question

GEMS moves to a fixed cap on mainnet (1,000,000 GEMS pre-minted, mint
authority to be destroyed at launch). The contract can no longer mint
rewards; it pays them from a reward pool deposited at initialize, and
fees flow back into that pool (recycling) or are destroyed (burns).

Two emission designs were simulated over 36 months under three growth
scenarios (pessimistic 50 launch players and shrinking; expected 200 and
growing 15%/mo with 10% churn; optimistic 1,000 and growing 25%/mo):

### A. Flat per-champion rates (the testnet design) — fails on a cap

Testnet rates (0.01–0.40 GEMS/min by station) scale emissions with the
champion population, which grows without bound as players summon. Even
scaled down 50x and with annual halvings, the expected scenario drains
the entire 850k pool in ~12 months, and champion payback at plausible
prices is 7–35 days — a bot invitation. No multiplier fixes this
structurally: linear-in-champions emissions always outrun a fixed pool.

### B. Budgeted pool share, pro-rata by staking weight — works

Each month the pool emits a fixed share of **itself** (modeled: 3.5%),
split among staked champions pro-rata by station weight (1/3/10/40,
mirroring the testnet ratios). Results:

| Scenario | Pool at month 36 | Sovereign payback (month 6, GEMS at 0.5 HTR) |
|---|---|---|
| Pessimistic | 33% | ~21 days (yield cap binds; see below) |
| Expected | 31% | **~173 days** |
| Optimistic | 64% | ~1,376 days |

Properties that make this the recommended design:

- **The pool can never exhaust** — it emits a share of what remains, and
  recycling tops it up. More players means the same budget spread
  thinner, not a faster drain.
- **Self-diluting yields are the bot defense.** A bot farm adds staking
  weight and dilutes its own returns; at expected growth, sovereign
  payback starts near 6 months and lengthens. Under flat rates a bot
  farm scales linearly.
- **Early adopters earn more** (fewer stakers sharing the budget), which
  is a launch incentive rather than a flaw — bounded by the yield cap.

## Refinements the simulation showed to be necessary

1. **Per-champion yield cap** (modeled: 0.5 GEMS/day per weight unit, so
   a sovereign caps at 20/day). Without it, a near-empty realm showers
   its last stakers (34+ GEMS/day per sovereign in the dying scenario).
   Unspent budget stays in the pool.
2. **Fees denominated in mining-days, not flat GEMS.** As yields dilute,
   flat fees become months of mining for late joiners (and in the
   optimistic run fees consume the entire circulating supply). Defining
   fees as `N days of the tier's current yield` (e.g. fusion = 7
   mining-days, temper first rite = 10) keeps effort parity constant
   forever and self-adjusts in both directions. The contract computes
   this from its own current emission rate.
3. **Burn shares**: temper keeps its 50% burn; a 20% burn on fusion fees
   is added in the model. Burns are the only true deflation against the
   cap; everything else recycles. On-chain `burned` counter, periodically
   matched by real melts from the operator-held melt authority
   (published transactions; melting also recovers the 1% HTR deposit).

## Supply and allocation

The 1M cap already minted is **sufficient** — under budget emission the
pool's lifetime is independent of its size (it emits shares, not flat
amounts). Size only affects granularity and optics. Suggested
allocation, to be published as the tokenomics policy:

- **850,000 — reward pool**, deposited into the arena contract at
  initialize. Only outlet: gameplay.
- **100,000 — Dozer liquidity.** Seeding note: the HTR paired against it
  sets the opening price (e.g. 15,000 HTR against 100k GEMS opens at
  0.15 HTR/GEMS). Seed modestly and let the market discover upward —
  a high opening price inflates early mining income and invites farming.
- **50,000 — tournaments, partnerships, marketing.** Time-locked or
  multisig if possible; published either way.
- **0 for sale. Mint authority destroyed at launch.**

## Secondary-market notes

- **GEMS on Dozer**: the assumed-price column above is why the yield cap
  and budget emission matter — at any price the market sets, payback
  stays in months. The "earned, never bought" story becomes a
  distribution policy: the pool's only outlet is play.
- **Champions on NileSwap as NFTs**: depends on the token-metadata PRD
  filed with Hathor (contract-minted tokens currently cannot carry
  media/NFT metadata). Also resolve: NFT convention is 1-unit tokens;
  cards are 100-unit tokens today because of the melt fee floor —
  revisit with Hathor when the PRD lands. Royalties are not enforceable
  on-chain on Hathor; if a marketplace fee share matters, it is a
  platform-level conversation with NileSwap.
- **Bazaar and NileSwap coexist**: the in-game Bazaar's 2% tithe is
  operator HTR revenue; NileSwap trading is free marketing and price
  discovery. Do not try to trap trading in-game.
